Mardi Gras

background The French Quarter of the city of New Orleans, the largest of the cities of the state of Louisiana in the south of the United States, becomes for two weeks a year a focus of unbridled parties and celebrations. The famous carnival, the Mardi Gras (Fat Tuesday in French), which is held in many places in the world, reaches in New Orleans a height of beauty and joy unmatched anywhere else. The wonderful carnival of New Orleans, which was first started in 1857 by French expatriates, is a global attraction for thousands of locals and tourists who plan their arrival in the city many months in advance. Due to the great demand that exists during the days of the festival for accommodation arrangements, it is recommended to prepare ahead of time. The carnival events reach their peak on " Fat Tuesday", (the day the meat-eating fast begins for 40 days until Easter) where two colorful carnival parades take place on St. Charles Street.The visit to the city during the carnival promises the visitor a unique cultural experience.


Mardi Gras Tips - Most of the Central Business District and French Quarter hotels require a four to five day minimum stay, and they steadily book blocks of rooms beginning in August. Make your reservations now! Hotels are often solidly booked by December, so reserve ahead of time. Check out recommended hotels close to the action or Mardi Gras packages. If they are sold out, we also recommend the hotels that are listed on Okay, so it's January, and you decide you want to visit New Orleans on Fat Tuesday. Should you even try? Yes! It is worth it, but just be prepared to spend a lot of time on the phone. Keep calling. Hotels have cancellations, and there are waiting lists. If you are persistent, you just may find a place somewhere. And remember, the parades and festivities start well before Fat Tuesday! Hotels often have rooms available when the parades first begin. This is a great time to visit New Orleans if you want to see some parades and get a great Mardi Gras experience without all the crowds.
